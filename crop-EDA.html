Crop Production Data Science

Introduction:
This project explores the detailed process of preparing the 20th-century food crop statistics dataset for analysis with a Decision Tree Regressor model. The dataset, encompassing crop statistics from 1900 to 2017, provides a rich historical view of agricultural productivity across different regions and crops. The preparation involved cleaning, imputing missing values, feature engineering, and ensuring data integrity to make it suitable for machine learning applications.

Data Cleaning and Preparation:
The process began with loading the dataset from an Excel file using the pandas library. Redundant and empty columns were removed, and key columns were renamed for clarity. Special attention was given to China-specific data due to its unique administrative divisions. Missing values were imputed, and the dataset was filtered to include years from 1930 to 2017. Zero values were replaced with calculated values where appropriate. Separate dataframes for cereals, maize, and wheat were created and processed to address unique characteristics and data discrepancies. Regions with excessive zero values were filtered out, and linear interpolation was applied to fill missing values.

Handling Specific Crops:
The dataset included separate processing for cereals, maize, and wheat to address unique characteristics and data discrepancies. For example, wheat data was corrected to include production values for 'wheat' where only 'winter wheat' or 'spring wheat' were recorded. This ensured a comprehensive representation of each crop's production.

Final Adjustments:
The datasets for maize, cereals, and wheat were concatenated into one. Zero values were replaced with NaN for 'production (tonnes)' and 'hectares (ha)' to prepare for final imputation. Yield values that were zero were recalculated to ensure consistency across the dataset.

Conclusion:
The preparation of the 20th-century food crop statistics dataset involved a series of meticulous steps designed to enhance the dataset's quality and suitability for machine learning models, specifically a Decision Tree Regressor. By cleaning the data, imputing missing values, and ensuring consistency across different crops and regions, we've laid a solid foundation for analyzing agricultural trends and predicting future productivity. The final dataset, saved as FINAL.csv, is now ready for in-depth analysis and modeling, promising insights into the factors driving crop production over the last century.
